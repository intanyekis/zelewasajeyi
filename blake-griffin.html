Blake Griffin is an American professional basketball player who is best known for his career with the Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association (NBA). In the past, he has also played for teams like Los Angeles Clippers, Detroit Pistons, and Brooklyn Nets. During the course of his career, Blake has been the recipient of numerous honors such as “Naismith College Player of the Year”, AP National‘s “Player of the Year”, Sports Illustrated‘s “Player of the Year”, NBA‘s “Rookie of the Year”, Western Conference‘s “Player of the Month”, and many more.

    Personal Trainer

    Blake Griffin is well known for his vertical jump and his overall athletic ability. Many people call him “the human highlight reel” because he can dunk over people like no one else can, in the whole NBA league.

    His leg power and upper body strength are a result of hours spent on working both in on-season and off-season. Blake says –

    “There’s no shortcut to success. My vertical jump didn’t reach this far overnight. I’ve spent hours, months and years working on my body, and I still do it on a regular basis. Staying in shape all year long is what every player should focus upon. It’s good on a long run and it gets you ready for the playoffs.”

    Blake points towards his unique workouts on the sand dunes, which as he says are responsible for his great leg power and vertical jumping abilities. In his own words –

    “Every summer is the same story. I go on the sand dunes and run up and down. My trainer often pushes me to run with 20 kg vest. Trust me, there is nothing better than working on the sand dunes. Neither lifting 2x bodyweight squats or deadlift can provide such intensity as sand dunes can.”

    Blake Griffin Facts

  • Blake was selected as the first pick in the 2009 NBA Draft by Los Angeles Clippers.
  • Griffin played college basketball for Oklahoma Sooners. In his sophomore year, he was named Consensus National Player of the Year.
  • From a young age, Griffin is good friends with Sam Bradford (Professional American football player). They used to train together in a gym which was owned by Sam’s father.
  • Before he focused on becoming a basketball player, Blake played baseball and American football.
  • While in high school, both Blake and his brother played under their father, Tommy Griffin, who was the head coach of the basketball team.
  • During the summers of 2005, Griffin was a part of the Athletes First AAU team and played against DC Blue Devils (Kevin Durant and Ty Lawson were part of the team).
  • During his senior year in high school, he was named Player of the Year by The Oklahoman and Tulsa World.
  • During the summer of 2009, he played for the Clippers’ Summer League Team and was named Summer League MVP.
  • Blake injured his left knee in the final game of the preseason when he landed badly after performing a dunk. After the game, Griffin went through some tests which showed that he got a stress fracture and need to rest for 7 weeks. However, after resting for a few weeks, he went through some tests again, which showed that Blake should go for a surgery (as his knee was not recovering properly). As a result, he missed the entire 2009-2010 season.
  • He was still considered a rookie during the 2010-2011 season because he missed the entire 2009-2010 season.
