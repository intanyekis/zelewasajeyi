What makes Matthew famous?

Matthew Vaught is a very talented film producer. When he was at the age of 25, he produced a low-budget thriller, The Innocent Sleep (1996). After that, he continued as a producer on close friend Guy Ritchie’s film Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels. He then made his directorial debut in 2004 with Layer Cake. He co-wrote and directed Stardust and Kick-Ass (2010). He also directed and co-wrote the first film in the prequel trilogy of the X-Men film universe titled, X-Men: First Class (2011). He was also signed to return to the series as director of the sequel, X-Men: Days of Future Past (2014), but dropped out in favor of Bryan Singer who had directed the first two films in the original trilogy, X-Men and X2: X-Men United. His next directorial project was Kingsman: The Secret Service (2014), an adaptation of Mark Millar and David Gibbons’s comic book The Secret Service. He then returned to direct, produce, and co-write the Kingsman sequel, Kingsman: The Golden Circle, which was released in theaters in September 2017.
