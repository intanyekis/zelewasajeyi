Leonard Bernstein net worth: Leonard Bernstein was an American composer, conductor, author, pianist, and music lecturer who had a net worth of $5 million at the time of his death in 1990. Leonard Bernstein was born in Lawrence, Massachusetts in August 1918 and passed away in October 1990. He served as the music director of the New York Philharmonic. Bernstein also composed or conducted music for productions including West Side Story, Candide, Peter Pan, On the Town, Wonderful Town, On the Waterfront, and Mass. He also authored several books including Findings, The Infinite Variety of Music, The Joy of Music, and Young People’s Concerts. Leonard Bernstein won Grammy Awards for Best Album for Children, Best Orchestral Performance, Best Choral Performance, Best Opera Recording, Best Classical Vocal Performance, Best Instrumental Soloist(s) Performance, Best Classical Contemporary Composition, Best Classical Album, and a Lifetime Achievement Award. He was also inducted into the American Theater Hall of Fame as well as the Television Hall of Fame. He was married to actress Felicia Montealegre from 1951 to 1978. Leonard Bernstein passed away on October 14, 1990 at 72 years old.
