SAN JOSE, CALIFORNIA: Steve Harwell, the former frontman for Smash Mouth has entered hospice care at 56 years old. Harwell, who retired from the band in 2021 is "on his deathbed" and has entered the last stage of liver failure, which was brought on by lifelong struggles with alcohol abuse, as per TMZ. 

Harwell is home and being cared for by his fiance, Robert Hayes, a manager for Smash Mouth, confirmed. 

A source said, "Unfortunately this is the case." "Steve is resting at home and being cared for by his fiance and hospice care."

Why did Steve Harwell quit 'Smash Mouth'?

Steve Harwell quit his band 'Smash Mouth' in 2021 following a 'chaotic' concert performance on the Big Sip stage. During the show, Harwell slurred his words, gave a Nazi salute on stage, and appeared to threaten the crowd.

A statement then released by Harwell's representative read, "Steve has been dealing with long-term medical issues over the last eight years, and during his last performance at the Big Sip stage, he suffered numerous symptoms directly linked with his current medical situation."

Further adding, "As of today, Steve will be retiring from Smash Mouth to focus on his physical and mental health." 

Who is 'Smash Mouth' frontman Steve Harwell?

Harwell was born in Santa Clara, California, and is 56 years old. He has had a successful career in the music industry and has gathered an estimated net worth of $2.5 million.

He is best known for setting up the band 'Smash Mouth' and for performing as its lead vocalist.
