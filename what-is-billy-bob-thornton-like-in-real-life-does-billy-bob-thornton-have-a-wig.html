Billy Bob Thornton was born on August 4, 1955, in Hot Springs, Arkansas, and was reared in a multiracial household. While his mother, Virginia Roberta, claimed to have psychic abilities, his father, William Raymond “Billy Ray” Thornton, worked as a high school history teacher and basketball coach until his passing in August 1974.

The other two brothers of Billy Bob, Jimmy Don, and John David, were both musicians.

As a child, Thornton led a nomadic lifestyle, residing in various towns across Arkansas like Alpine, Malvern, and Mount Holly. He was raised in a modest, no-frills shack and a Methodist family.

He applied to play baseball for the Kansas City Royals in 1973 after graduating from Malvern High School, but the franchise rejected him due to an injury.

Before enrolling in the psychology department at Henderson State University, he first spent a brief time working for the Arkansas State Transportation Department. But after only two semesters, he dropped out.

In the middle of the 1980s, Thornton relocated to Los Angeles to begin a challenging acting career. He pursued acting opportunities while working odd jobs including fast restaurant management, telemarketing, and offshore wind farms. Thornton performed drums and sang with the South African rock band Jack Hammer, showcasing his musical talents.
